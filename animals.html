The osprey (Pandion haliaetus), also known as halieto, guincho or fisherman's hawk, is a species of accipitriform bird of the Pandionidae family. It is a medium-sized raptor that appears on all continents, except Antarctica, although in South America it is only a non-nesting migrant. Some classifications place it as the only member of the genus Pandion, while others consider a second species within this genus, Pandion cristatus. The osprey was one of many species described by Charles Linnaeus in his 18th-century work, Systema naturæ, and named Falco haliætus. The genus Pandion, the only member of the family Pandionidae, was described by the French zoologist Marie Jules César Savigny in 1809 and takes the name of the mythical Greek king, Pandion.6910 The osprey differs in several respects from other diurnal birds of prey. The toes are of equal length, their tarsi are reticulated and their claws are rounded. Ospreys and owls are the only birds of prey whose outer toes are reversible, allowing them to grasp prey with two toes in front and two toes behind. This is particularly useful when catching slippery fish

The ardeidae (Ardeidae) are a family of Pelecaniform birds that includes more than sixty species, known generically as herons throughout the Hispanic world. The ardeids were previously classified within the order of the ciconiiformes. They are wading birds, and some species can measure up to 85 cm tall. Generally its plumage is white, its beak yellow and its long gray legs, although the plumage varies according to the season of the year. They feed on fish, crustaceans and small amphibians, and their habitat is swampy areas or near lakes around the world. They are generally solitary, but it is normal to see them gathered in community in the breeding season. They nest in tall trees, where they make rough nests, practically flat, and lay up to six eggs. They are distributed worldwide and are among the most abundant continental birds in the world, second only to passerines. Herons are important in the control of invasive species such as rainbow trout (Oncorhynchus mykiss).
