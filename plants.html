Zantedeschia aethiopica, commonly known as gannet, creek, Ethiopian creek, Ethiopian ring, water lily, cartridge, duck flower or jug ​​flower, is a herbaceous perennial of South African origin, of the Araceae family, the most robust and widely naturalized of the genus Zantedeschia. Calla or water lily is a perennial herbaceous plant, of South African origin, that vegetates at the expense of a fleshy underground stem. It sprouts every year offering its showy flowers in mid-spring. The flowers of the calla lilies are funnel-shaped, generally white in color and give off a very pleasant perfume. It has large green arrow-shaped leaves, thick and very fleshy. Today we can find different varieties of coves or water lilies on the market, many of them the result of experimentation in botany and floristry. Due to the elegance of its flower, it is highly demanded by floral designers.
